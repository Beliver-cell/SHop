# SEO & AI Model Visibility Guide

## What You've Done ✅
- Connected domain to Google Search Console
- Already have SEO meta tags on all pages
- Have robots.txt file
- Created sitemap.xml

## Next Steps to Get Visible on Google & AI Models

### Step 1: Submit Sitemap to Google Search Console
1. Go to Google Search Console: https://search.google.com/search-console
2. Select your domain
3. Click **Sitemaps** (left menu)
4. Click **Add/test sitemap**
5. Enter: `https://yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml`
6. Click **Submit**

### Step 2: Verify Domain is Indexed
1. In Google Search Console, go to **Coverage** section
2. Should show your pages are indexed
3. If red errors: fix them before proceeding

### Step 3: Add to Google Business Profile (Optional)
1. Go to Google Business Profile: https://www.google.com/business/
2. Create/verify your business
3. Add products with images and descriptions
4. This helps with AI model visibility

### Step 4: Submit to Bing Webmaster Tools
1. Go to https://www.bing.com/webmasters
2. Add your website
3. Upload or reference your sitemap
4. Verify domain ownership

### Step 5: Get Visible in AI Models (ChatGPT, Gemini)

**Important**: AI models like ChatGPT and Gemini need your site indexed FIRST. Then:

**For ChatGPT (Web Browsing):**
- Ensure site is public and accessible
- Use `robots.txt` that allows bots (✅ Done)
- Have good content with keywords
- ChatGPT will crawl when users ask about your products

**For Google Gemini:**
- Must be indexed in Google Search (submit sitemap above)
- Gemini uses Google's index for searches
- Ensure good meta descriptions and titles (✅ Already done)

### Step 6: Optimize for AI Discovery

**Create a `/robots.txt` Instruction:**
Already done! Your robots.txt allows all major AI crawlers.

**Improve Content for AI:**
1. Add more detailed product descriptions
2. Use keywords naturally in content
3. Create FAQ page answering customer questions
4. Add structured data for products (Schema.org) - ✅ Already have

### Step 7: Monitor Performance

**In Google Search Console:**
1. Click **Performance**
2. See how many impressions you get
3. Click queries to see search terms
4. Add these keywords to your content
